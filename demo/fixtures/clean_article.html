Transformer Architectures: A Survey

Transformer models have become the dominant architecture in natural language processing since their introduction in "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al., 2017). The core innovation — the self-attention mechanism — allows the model to weigh the relevance of each token against every other token in a sequence, enabling rich contextual representations without recurrence.

Subsequent work scaled these ideas dramatically. BERT introduced bidirectional pre-training on masked language modeling, while GPT models adopted a left-to-right causal objective suited to generation. Both families share the same underlying attention backbone, differing mainly in training objective and downstream use.

More recent architectures have explored sparse attention (Longformer, BigBird) to handle longer sequences, mixture-of-experts layers (Switch Transformer, Mixtral) to increase parameter count without proportional compute costs, and retrieval-augmented approaches (RAG, Atlas) that separate parametric and non-parametric memory.

Benchmark performance on GLUE, SuperGLUE, and MMLU has improved steadily across generations, though researchers note that benchmark saturation has made evaluating genuine reasoning harder over time.
