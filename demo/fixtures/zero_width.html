Tokenization in Large Language Models

Byte-pair encoding (BPE) is the dominant tokenization strategy for modern language models. Starting from a character-level vocabulary, BPE iteratively merges the most frequent adjacent pair of tokens until a target vocabulary size is reached.

SentencePiece and tiktoken are the most widely deployed BPE implementations. Vocabulary size typically ranges from 32k to 128k tokens; larger vocabularies reduce sequence length but increase embedding table size and the difficulty of learning rare token representations.
